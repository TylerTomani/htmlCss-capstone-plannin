Starting and Ending Anchors

Metatcharacter Meaning
^ Start of string/line
$ End of string/line
\A Start of string, never end of line
\Z End of string, never end of line

we use the ^ anchor to ensure the word is the first word in the string

/^apple/ or /\Aapple/

we use the $ to ensure the word is at the end of the string

/apple$/ or /apple\Z/


The ^ and $ are supported in all regex tools, but \A and \Z are not

good example

searching emails in right format

/\w@\w\.[a-z]{3}/

but would still validate if something was after ".com"

to ensure the email is the right format we should add "^" and "$"

/^\w+@\w\.[a-z]{3}$/
